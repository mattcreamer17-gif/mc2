THE FRAME

Three layers. One conversation.

Most of the work your clients label "AI" splits three ways. About 60% is traditional code and database work. 30% is rule-based logic that belongs in a workflow engine. 10% is genuine AI. When you sell "AI-first platforms," your buyers hear one thing and your delivery team scopes another. The fix is a clear way to show the buyer, on the first call, which layer each piece of their problem sits on.

Eduba calls this computational orchestration. A short methodology document plus a working session. Your team applies it once, the template becomes yours.
